Nalanda Mahavihara

Nalanda Mahavihara, located in present-day Bihar, India, was one of the ancient world's most renowned centers of learning and scholarship. Established around the 5th century CE during the Gupta Empire, it attracted scholars from across Asia, including China, Korea, Japan, Tibet, Mongolia, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. The university was renowned for its vast library, which housed thousands of manuscripts, and its curriculum covered a wide range of subjects, including Buddhism, philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and logic. Nalanda Mahavihara flourished under the patronage of various Indian dynasties until it was destroyed in the 12th century by the Turkish invader Bakhtiyar Khilji. Today, the ruins of Nalanda are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, preserving the legacy of this iconic educational institution.

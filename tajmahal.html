Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1632 and 1653 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is a symbol of eternal love. This white marble mausoleum is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and exquisite inlay work using semi-precious stones. Set on the banks of the Yamuna River, the Taj Mahal is surrounded by lush gardens and reflects a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles, making it a globally admired masterpiece.

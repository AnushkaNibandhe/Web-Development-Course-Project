Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves, located on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbour, Maharashtra, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, inscribed in 1987. These caves are a group of rock-cut temples dedicated primarily to Lord Shiva and date back to the 5th to 8th centuries CE. The island was originally called Gharapuri, meaning the "City of Caves," and was later renamed by the Portuguese, who discovered a large stone elephant statue near the site. The caves consist of two main groups: five Hindu caves and two smaller Buddhist caves. The most prominent feature is the massive three-headed sculpture of Lord Shiva, known as Trimurti, symbolizing Shiva as the Creator, Preserver, and Destroyer.

Mahabodhi Temple

The Mahabodhi Temple Complex in Bodh Gaya, Bihar, is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites for Buddhists worldwide. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2002, it marks the sacred spot where Prince Siddhartha Gautama attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree around 500 BCE and became the Buddha. The site features a magnificent 50-meter-high pyramidal temple, originally established by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE and later reconstructed during the Gupta period. The temple is adorned with intricate carvings depicting Buddhaâ€™s life and teachings. Central to the complex is the Vajrasana, or Diamond Throne, where Buddha meditated, and the Bodhi Tree, a direct descendant of the original tree. Pilgrims from all over the world visit the site to meditate under the tree, circumambulate the temple, and pay homage to the Sacred Lotus Pond, adding to the profound spiritual and historical significance of this iconic monument.

Buddhist monuments at sanchi

The Buddhist Monuments of Sanchi, in Madhya Pradesh, India, date back to the 3rd century BCE, built by Emperor Ashoka. The site features the Great Stupa, intricately carved gateways, smaller stupas, monasteries, and an Ashokan pillar. It is one of the oldest and best-preserved Buddhist sites, depicting early Buddhist art and architecture. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site (1989), Sanchi remains a major center of Buddhist heritage and pilgrimage.

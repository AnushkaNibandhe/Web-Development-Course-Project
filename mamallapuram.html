Mamallapuram

Mamallapuram, also known as Mahabalipuram, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Tamil Nadu, India, renowned for its ancient rock-cut architecture and stone carvings. Built between the 7th and 8th centuries under the Pallava dynasty, it showcases exceptional craftsmanship through its iconic structures, such as the Shore Temple, Pancha Rathas (Five Chariots), and the massive bas-relief, "Descent of the Ganges." The site features cave temples, monolithic shrines, and intricately carved sculptures that reflect scenes from Hindu mythology. Set along the Bay of Bengal, Mamallapuram blends artistic heritage with natural beauty, attracting historians, pilgrims, and tourists from around the world.

Raani ki Vaav

Located in Patan, Gujarat, Rani-ki-Vaav is an 11th-century stepwell built by Queen Udayamati in memory of King Bhimdev I of the Solanki dynasty. This inverted temple-like structure honors the sanctity of water, descending seven intricately carved levels adorned with over 1,500 sculptures. The carvings depict Lord Vishnu's avatars, celestial beings, and mythological tales, showcasing the Solanki era's artistic brilliance. Beyond its architectural beauty, the stepwell served as a water management system and a refuge during Gujaratâ€™s hot summers. Buried under silt for centuries, it was excavated and restored in the 20th century, now standing as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

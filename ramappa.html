Ramappa Temple

The Ramappa Temple, also known as the Rudreshwara Temple, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Palampet, Telangana, India. Built in the 13th century during the reign of the Kakatiya dynasty, it is a stunning example of Kakatiya architecture and craftsmanship. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is named after its chief sculptor, Ramappa. It features intricate carvings, delicately sculpted pillars, and a unique floating brick construction used for its lightweight shikhara (tower).

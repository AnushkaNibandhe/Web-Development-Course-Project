Khajuraho

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments, located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning medieval Hindu and Jain temples. Built between 950 and 1050 CE during the rule of the Chandela dynasty, these temples are celebrated for their intricate carvings and exquisite sculptures. The art depicts various themes, including spirituality, daily life, mythology, and love, with some sculptures portraying sensual and erotic themes. Of the original 85 temples, around 20 have survived, showcasing a perfect blend of architectural grandeur and artistic brilliance. The temples are set against the backdrop of lush greenery, making Khajuraho a significant cultural and historical destination.

Dholavira: A Harappan City

The Dholavira: A Harappan City, located in the Kutch district of Gujarat, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site inscribed in 2021. This archaeological marvel is one of the most prominent cities of the Indus Valley Civilization, dating back to approximately 3000 BCE to 1500 BCE. Dholavira is unique for its meticulous city layout, which includes a citadel, middle town, and lower town, all fortified with massive stone walls. The site is renowned for its sophisticated water management system, featuring reservoirs, large public spaces, unique burial practices, stepwells, and channels, which reflect the inhabitants' ingenuity in sustaining life in an arid region and the discovery of a signboard inscribed with the Harappan script, highlighting their communication methods. The city's advanced infrastructure, combined with its cultural and economic significance, makes Dholavira a key site in understanding the ancient Harappan civilization and its legacy.

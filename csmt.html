Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

This historic railway station in Mumbai, formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is an iconic symbol of Indiaâ€™s colonial past and architectural heritage. Completed in 1888, it was designed by British architect Frederick William Stevens, blending Victorian Gothic Revival architecture with traditional Indian elements. The station is renowned for its grand stone dome, pointed arches, ornate carvings, and stained-glass windows, which give it a palace-like appearance. As a bustling transportation hub, it played a key role in the development of Mumbai as a major port city. Today, the terminus stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, representing the fusion of two distinct architectural traditions and serving as a vital landmark in the city.

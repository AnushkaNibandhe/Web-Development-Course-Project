Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering historical monument located in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India. Standing at 73 meters (240 feet), it is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The construction of the minaret began in 1193 under the rule of Qutb-ud-Din Aibak, the first ruler of the Delhi Sultanate, and was later completed by his successor, Iltutmish. The Qutub Minar is made of red sandstone and is adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions in Arabic, showcasing Islamic architectural styles. It was originally built to celebrate the victory of the Muslim forces over the Delhi Rajputs and to serve as a minaret for the nearby mosque. The monument is a prominent symbol of Delhi's rich historical and cultural heritage, attracting millions of tourists annually.

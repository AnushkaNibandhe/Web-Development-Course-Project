Kumbhalgarh Fort

Located in the Rajsamand district of Rajasthan, Kumbhalgarh Fort is a remarkable example of Rajput military architecture and one of the largest forts in India. Built in the 15th century by Rana Kumbha, it served as the strategic stronghold of the Mewar kingdom. The fort is famous for its massive walls, which stretch over 36 kilometers and are the second-longest continuous fortification in the world, after the Great Wall of China. Within the fort's boundaries lie several temples, including the Kumbheshwar Temple, as well as palaces and gardens that showcase the splendor of Rajput architecture.

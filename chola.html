Chola Temples, Tamil Nadu

The Chola Temples of Tamil Nadu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are remarkable examples of Dravidian architecture and represent the zenith of the Chola dynasty's artistic and engineering prowess. Built between the 10th and 12th centuries, the site includes the Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur, the Brihadeeswarar Temple in Gangaikonda Cholapuram, and the Airavatesvara Temple in Darasuram. These temples are renowned for their towering vimanas (temple towers), intricately carved sculptures, and exquisite bronze statues. Dedicated primarily to Lord Shiva, they showcase the Cholas' devotion to art, spirituality, and architectural innovation. These temples remain active centers of worship and cultural heritage.

Monuments at Hampi, Karnataka

Hampi, located in the state of Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rich history and architectural splendor. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire (14thâ€“16th centuries), Hampi is dotted with stunning temples, grand palaces, and impressive stone structures. Surrounded by boulder-strewn landscapes and the Tungabhadra River, the site is both a spiritual and historical marvel. Iconic landmarks include the Virupaksha Temple, the stone chariot at the Vittala Temple, and the massive monolithic sculptures such as the Nandi and Narasimha. Hampi attracts history enthusiasts, pilgrims, and backpackers alike, offering a glimpse into the grandeur of a bygone era.
